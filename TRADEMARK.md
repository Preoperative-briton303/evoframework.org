# Trademark Policy - evo / evoframework

`evo` and `evoframework` are trademarks of Just a Nerd, used in
commerce since the project's first public publication.

This trademark policy is published at the root of every public evo-*
repository so that adopters, plugin authors, vendor distributions,
and the wider community know what they may and may not do with the
marks. It complements the licensing terms in `LICENSE` (the licence
governs the use of the source code; this policy governs the use of
the project's name and visual identity).

## Permitted uses

Any of the following are explicitly permitted without prior
permission:

- Factual reference. "Built on evo", "evo-compatible", "this plugin
  runs on the evo framework", "we use evo internally for X".
- Accurate compatibility statements. "Compatible with evo 0.1.13",
  "tested with evo on Raspberry Pi 5", "ships an evo plugin under
  namespace `com.example.dsp`".
- Educational and editorial use. Articles, blog posts, talks,
  tutorials, books, conference presentations, academic papers, and
  similar discussion of the project.
- Fair-use commentary, criticism, and parody, as permitted by
  applicable law in the relevant jurisdiction.
- Pull requests, issues, and contributions referencing the project
  by name in their natural context.

When making a factual reference or compatibility statement, please
write `evo` (or `evoframework`) in lowercase, and include the
trademark symbol on first prominent use: `evo(TM)` or
`evoframework(TM)`.

## Forbidden uses

The following uses of the marks are NOT permitted without prior
written agreement from the trademark holder:

- Branding products with the mark. Naming a product, service,
  hardware device, software offering, or company using `evo`,
  `evoframework`, or any confusingly-similar derivative
  (`evo-pro`, `evo+`, `EvoFramework`, `Evosphere`, `evo2`, etc.) as
  a primary or material part of the name.
- Domain names containing the mark. Registering or using a domain
  name that incorporates `evo` or `evoframework` as a primary token
  (e.g. `evoframework.io`, `getevo.com`, `evo-cloud.net`).
  Subdomains of unrelated domains that incidentally use the term in
  factual reference are permitted.
- App store / marketplace listings using the mark. Publishing a paid
  or free app to a public marketplace whose listing title includes
  `evo` or `evoframework` as a primary identifier.
- Deceptive use suggesting affiliation, sponsorship, or endorsement.
  Using the marks in a way that would lead a reasonable observer to
  conclude that the trademark holder endorses, supports, sponsors,
  or is affiliated with a third-party product or service when no
  such relationship exists.
- Derivative marks, logos, or visual identity. Modified versions of
  any logo or visual identity associated with the project,
  derivative wordmarks, or visual elements designed to suggest a
  formal relationship with the project.
- Use as a generic term. Use of the mark as a verb ("to evo
  something"), as a generic noun for a class of software ("an
  evo"), or in any way that contributes to dilution of the mark.

## Permission requests

If a proposed use of the marks is not clearly permitted under
"Permitted uses" above, please open an issue at the canonical
project repository titled `[trademark]` followed by a brief
description of the proposed use. The trademark holder will respond
with a permission, refusal, or request for additional information
within a reasonable time.

## Enforcement and good-faith use

This policy is intended to be predictable and adopter-friendly. The
trademark holder reserves the right to enforce against uses that
meaningfully damage the project's reputation, mislead the community,
or commercially exploit the marks without authorisation. Routine
community use, factual reference, accurate compatibility statements,
and educational use are explicitly welcomed and will not be
challenged.

## Future tightening

Formal trademark registration, additional jurisdictions, and
graphical-mark protection are tracked separately and may be added to
this policy as the project's trajectory justifies the cost of
registration and counsel engagement.
